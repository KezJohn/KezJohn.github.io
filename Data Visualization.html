Spill Incidents in New York State (2000–2023)

This project was completed during my sophomore year, with the illustrations presented here being a portion of the overall project and presentation. The report analyzes spill incidents across New York State from 2000 to 2023, using data sourced from data.ny.gov: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/spill-incidents. It highlights trends, geographic patterns, and material-specific spill incidents. By utilizing Tableau's visualization features, I aimed to identify insights and predict future spill trends.

Symbol Map: Spill Distribution Across Counties

This visualization highlights the geographical distribution of spills across New York State from 2000 to 2023. Counties like Westchester (954), New York (703), Saratoga (666), and Onondaga (508) reported the highest incidents, while Chenango (48), Tompkins (61), Cattaraugus (79), Schoharie (67), and Herkimer (92) had the lowest. This map helps identify high-risk areas, enabling targeted preventive measures and resource allocation.

Trend Analysis: Spill Incidents Over Time

The trend analysis, displayed as a line graph, illustrates the fluctuation of spill incidents from 2000 to 2023. The categories are color-coded: green for petroleum, blue for hazardous materials, red for oxygenates, and orange for others. Over the years, the graph emphasizes a declining trend in spill incidents, with petroleum spills being the most prominent. The sharp reduction in spills after 2020 indicates enhanced safety measures, though petroleum continues to require careful monitoring. Other materials consistently show low spill counts, demonstrating effective handling practices.

Dual Map: Spill Data for 2022

While the trend analysis revealed a rapid decline in disasters, the 2022 dual map highlights their continued significant impact on counties. Westchester experienced no incidents, while Onondaga reported the highest number (210), largely attributed to petroleum spills. Meanwhile, counties like Cattaraugus (2) and Ontario (1) recorded minimal spills caused by hazardous materials.

Forecast Analysis: Future Spill Trends (2023–2025)

The forecast analysis predicts a gradual reduction in spills from 2023 to 2025 but notes potential slight increases due to unforeseen events. Petroleum is projected to remain the most common material involved, with an estimated count of 9,056 spills in 2025. This chart underscores the need for ongoing vigilance and proactive measures despite overall improvements.

Summary

In conclusion, spill incidents across New York State from 2000 to 2023 posed significant risks to counties like Westchester and Onondaga. The trend analysis highlights a notable decline in overall incidents, particularly after 2020, demonstrating the impact of enhanced safety measures and regulations. However, the dual map for 2022 reveals that Onondaga reported the highest number of spills (210), primarily due to petroleum, which remains a critical concern. Forecasts indicate a potential increase, with an estimated 9,056 petroleum-related spills in 2025, driven by unpredictable factors. This emphasizes petroleum's pivotal role in spill incidents, while hazardous materials, though minimal, still contribute to the issue.With diligent efforts from state and county authorities, these predictions can be mitigated, ensuring further progress in spill prevention and environmental safety.

Skincare Product and Brand Analysis (Power BI)

The report examines spending patterns across skin types and brand performance by price and ratings. Using data-driven insights, it highlights affordability trends, product accessibility, and consumer preferences for quality over cost. By leveraging visual analysis, the study provides a comprehensive overview of pricing and ratings across various skincare categories.

Price Segmentation

This graph highlights spending patterns and affordability across different skin types. Consumers with normal skin show flexibility, purchasing products across both affordable and luxurious price ranges. Those with combination skin have the widest spending capacity, choosing products in any price category. In contrast, individuals with oily skin predominantly purchase affordable products, with average costs below $50, and have limited options in higher price ranges. Meanwhile, dry skin consumers, like those with combination skin, can afford mid-range products priced between $50 and $100. Notably, all skin types have access to products under $50, ensuring affordability for a broad consumer base. However, oily skin stands out as the only group with minimal options in the luxury price range. These findings emphasize the importance of offering products at various price points to cater to the diverse needs of consumers based on their skin type.

Analyzing Product Quality and Affordability Across Brands

This graph illustrates the relationship between brand ratings and product prices across different categories. For skin treatments, Fresh has the highest price, exceeding $150, but its rating is below 4.3. Conversely, Biossance offers treatments in the $120 price range with a rating of 4.5. Notably, Charlotte Tilbury stands out with the highest rating of 4.9 for cleansers, while Kate Somerville’s moisturizer has a moderate rating of 4.2. Treatments tend to have higher prices and relatively lower ratings, whereas moisturizers are priced under $120 and maintain moderate ratings. Tom Ford’s sun protection achieves a 4.6 rating at a price below $80. Overall, Sephora-branded products remain affordable, priced under $40, while offering ratings up to 4.6. Additionally, cleansers from brands like Dior, Dr. Dennis, and Drunk Elephant have lower prices and average ratings above 4.5. This analysis highlights that customers prioritize quality over price when making purchasing decisions.

2022 Chronic Disease Analysis (Excel)

This project delves into the analysis of chronic diseases using patient health data from 2022. The primary focus is on identifying trends based on Body Mass Index (BMI) and diabetes status while evaluating associated risk categories. The aim is to assist healthcare professionals in making informed decisions by presenting clear visualizations of data and offering actionable insights. The analysis is categorized by gender and age group, providing a comprehensive view of health risks in different demographics.

According to the 2022 database, older adults show a higher predisposition to prediabetes, with 337 men and 242 women already diagnosed with diabetes. The data highlights that men are nearly twice as likely to develop diabetes compared to women. This emphasizes the need for targeted interventions, especially for older men.

The table highlights that older adults face higher cholesterol-related risks. Gender differences are minimal: 101 women and 116 men are at risk, with 56 women and 53 men at dangerous levels, showing similar vulnerability in old age.

Detailed Analysis of BMI, Diabetes, and Cholesterol Trends

The data highlights significant chronic disease concerns, particularly in specific demographics. Among the "Old Age" group, obesity is alarmingly high, with 200 cases, and overweight cases are equally distributed between men and women. Middle-aged males emerge as a high-risk group, with a noticeable trend of morbid obesity, posing severe health risks and requiring immediate intervention. Additionally, men account for approximately 60% of obesity and diabetes cases, underscoring a pronounced gender disparity in chronic disease prevalence. While older adults and middle-aged males face the highest burden of chronic conditions, young adults also exhibit diabetes issues, though at comparatively lower levels. This early emergence of diabetes in younger demographics warrants closer monitoring to prevent escalation. Overall, the analysis identifies old-age individuals and middle-aged males as the most vulnerable, emphasizing the need for targeted health strategies while keeping an eye on emerging risks in younger populations.
